# Meditation Practice

## Daily Schedule

- Pick at least 2 specific times every day, to meditate for 15-30m. Setting an alarm helps, for consistency. Find what works best for you.

## What Doesn't Work

- Doing 1-10m per day is pointless. Your mind is like a glass of water with sediments, it needs time for those sediments to settle to the bottom. This is why skilled meditators recommend longer sessions. Dipa Ma recommends 4h / day, 2h sit at dawn, 2h in the evening. 1-2h per day seems to be the recommended amount, but 20-45m / day is a good starting point.
- Trying to start out too long / day will fail, because it requires a lot of motivation. We want to gradually build a habit, not rely on motivation.

## Preparation Before Meditating on Your Subject

- Mindful breath that you will succeed, and are on the same path as the Buddha and other arahants.
- Mindful breath of loving-kindness towards all beings.
- Mindful breath of death, and the impurity of your body.

## Noble Eightfold Path

Acronym: VILASCEM (view, intention, livelihood, action, speech, concentration, effort, mindfulness)

- Right view
  - Learning, Study, 4 noble truths,
  - 3 marks.
  - Dependent arising
  - Knowing what is wholesome and unwholesome kamma, and its fruits
  - 10 courses of unwholesome / wholesome kamma, body speech and mind
    - Destroying life
    - Stealing
    - Sensual pursuits
    - Lying
    - Slanderous speech
    - Harsh speech
    - Idle chatter
    - Covetousness
    - Ill will
    - Wrong view
- Counteracts delusion
- Right intention
  - 3 wrong intentions: greed, hatred / aversion, harmfulness / cruelty
  - Opposites: renunciation, loving-kindness, harmlessness
- Right speech
  - 4 wrong types: false, divisive/disharmony, harsh, frivilous/idle/vain
  - Opposites: truthful, harmonious / unifying, soft / gentle, purposeful
- Right action
  - No killing, stealing/exploiting, sexual misconduct,
  - Opposites: Preserving / protecting life, Respecting others belongings, sexual honesty
- Right livelihood
  - No harm to others or one's self or mind
  - 6: No dealing in meat, poisons, weapons, slavery/prostitution, intoxicants, usury
  - No trickery or dishonesty
- Right effort
  - Energy, work, exertion, determination.
  - 4 aspects.
    - Preventing unarisen unwholesome states (greed, hatred, delusion).
    - Abandon arisen unwholesome states. (5 hindrances)
    - Developing unarisen wholesome ones (8 factors of this path, 4 foundations of mindfulness, 7 factors of enlightenment).
    - Develop / maintain / cultivate existing wholesome states.
  - Gardener analogy : Weed killer to prevent weeds from growing, tearing up the existing weeds by the roots, planting and watering seeds of good karma, protecting and cultivating the existing plants.
  - Weed out the hindrances, cultivate the awakening factors.
  - See ODISC above
- Right mindfulness / insight meditation
  - Awareness/attention to the present, without elaborating, judging, liking/disliking, grasping, or evaluating with a goal.
  - 4 foundations: body (breathing, postures, parts), feeling, states of mind, universal laws
  - When enough concentration is built up to mindfulness, panna develops, direct seeing into the 4 noble truths, from moment to moment. Seeing becomes direct and immediate.
  - Looks at the 5 aggregates, and finds that the 3 marks apply to them. Watches them arise and pass away, and finds that since they all dissolve, no true security or happiness can be found in them.
  - Eventually equanimity arises. Even though the 3 marks apply to everything, he has no fear or disgust or sorrow at this process.
  - Insight meditation is the unique discovery of the Buddha.
  - Path of insight - focuses on insight meditation first (4 foundations of mindfulness), then build some concentration, then apply it back to insight.
    - Momentary concentration - moment by moment we look at the 4 foundations, and this snowballs and builds, until we reach a high level.
- Right concentration / serenity meditation
  - Wholesome one-pointedness, collected, stilled, focused, tranquil, without disturbances or hindrances.
  - Focuses on a single object: breath, circular disks of 4 elements, or colored disks of the primary colors, parts of the body, Buddha Dhamma Sangha, the divine abodes.
  - Brings the mind back again and again to the object.
  - 4 jhanas
  - It will not eliminate latent defilements, without insight.
  - Only used as a powerful tool to develop insight.
  - Used in conjunction with right view, but instead of a conceptual understanding, it's a vision of the dhamma. Instead of idea, make the dhamma a perception.
  - Gain concentration, apply it to right mindfulness, repeat.
  - Gradually insight arises, it turns into panna - the liberation wisdom of seeing into the 4 noble truths.
  - It becomes direct and immediate.
  - Select a single object: breath. Focus single object, excluding all mental distractions, notes them briefly, lets them go, and goes back to subject.
  - 5 hindrances may come up.
  - Path of serenity - focuses on serenity meditation, to a high level, then uses it as a tool to apply it to insight.

### 4 Perversions of Ignorance (Applies to Perception, Thinking, and Views)

- Seeing the unattractive as attractive (IE the body)
- Regarding dukkha and worldly pleasures, as capable of providing true happiness.
- Regarding the impermanent as permanent.
- Seeing what is not self / conditioned, as our self / ego.

### How to Prevent Craving

- Breaking the link between feeling and craving in the most important link in the dependent origination chain.
- To do this, be mindful of pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral feelings, and note that they are impermanent, suffering, and conditioned/not me, not mine, not myself.
- Don't buy into them, get attached to them, or crave them.

## Three Marks / Universal Characteristics

All phenomena, from microbes to empires to world systems to ideas, have these three characteristics.

- Impermanent
  - The root characteristic.
  - Is the basis for the other two.
  - 2 types
    - Gross - 4 stages of development / evolution
      - Starts as undifferentiated matter.
      - Evolves to maximum differentiation.
      - Starts to disintegrate / break up.
      - Reaches disintegration / destruction.
      - Repeat
    - Subtle -- TODO
- Suffering / unsatisfactoriness
  - 3 types
    - Frequent - union with the unpleasant, separation from the pleasant, not getting what we want.
    - Occasional - birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, grief, lamentation, loss, pain, despair
    - Constant - 5 aggregates of clinging. Even pleasurable things are suffering due to the three marks. Momentary events without any real core.
  - 3 levels (3 marks)
    - Ordinary suffering - experienced suffering, bodily or mental.
    - Suffering due to change - even pleasant experiences are unsatisfactory, since they are bound to pass away, and enjoying them creates the perversion of permanence.
    - Suffering due conditionality / no-self. IE, the 5 aggregates. IE all phenomena are conditioned in a web, and nothing exists on its own.
- No-self / conditioned existence
  - No inner nucleus of selfhood behind the 5 aggregates.
  - Bhikkhu bodhi's 4 false notions of self list (SPUC)
    - Permanence - Having definable duration, and unchanged during that duration.
    - Simplicity - not compounded, divisible or breakable into parts
    - Unconditioned - self sufficient, not dependent on anything. We can't identify with it or call it "us" if its conditioned
    - Controllable - If its us, then we can exercise mastery / control over it. Never any conflict between what we want ourselves to be, and what it is.
  - Life is a process, a transfer of energy, a candle's flame being passed to new candles (in this life, and in rebirth).

First we understand these intellectually, then experiencing them directly / watching them.

## 4 Noble Truths

All Buddha's discoveries (dhammas) fit into the 4 noble truths.

This is the medical formula:

- Diagnosis, cause, prognosis, medicine
- Problem, cause, solution, path to solution

- Suffering
  - Unsatisfactory nature of conditioned / impermanent existence.
  - Misalignment between what we want, permanent happiness, and the reality, impermanence.
  - 3 classifications:
    - Infrequent (grief, loss, sickness)
    - Frequent (not getting what you want)
    - Persistent: unsatisfactoriness
  - 3 levels (IE the 3 marks)
    - Felt suffering (bodily, mental).
    - Suffering due to change (even pleasant experience).
      - Even pleasant feelings are unsatisfactory, because they are bound to pass away. Temporary states of pleasure are suffering.
    - Conditioned suffering (5 aggregates).
- Its origin: Craving
  - 3 roots: greed, hatred, delusion
- Its cessation: Nibbana
  - The unconditioned state
  - The deathless state
  - Elimination of fetters and defilements
  - The blowing out / extinguishing of the fire of self, and its fuels of greed, hatred, and delusion
  - The unbinding / breaking of the chains / 10 fetters
- The way to its cessation: Noble eightfold path

### Other seven contemplations of the four foundations

Consider any of the four as:

1. as impermanent and not as permanent
2. as painful and not as pleasurable
3. as not-self and not as a self
4. he turns away and is not delighted by it
5. he is dispassionate and does not crave
6. he causes cessation of it and not origination
7. he relinquishes and does not grasp

Contemplating it as impermanent, he abandons the notion of permanency; contemplating it as painful, he abandons the notion of pleasure; contemplating it as not-self, he abandons the notion of a self; by turning away he abandons delight; by being dispassionate he abandons greed; by causing cessation he abandons origination; by relinquishing he abandons grasping.
